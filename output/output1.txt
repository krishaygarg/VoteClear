**Candidate Name: Donald Trump**

**Economic Policy: This category includes government actions aimed at influencing the economy's performance and stability. It covers areas like taxation, government spending, trade regulations, monetary policy, jobs, and inflation.:**
Donald Trump's economic policy stances emphasize combating inflation, which he pledges to "end" and make America affordable again. He aims to reduce prices for groceries, cars, and housing, and has proposed a temporary cap on credit card interest rates at 10%. To address housing costs, he plans to eliminate regulations to cut the cost of new homes in half. On trade, Trump has historically favored tariffs, a policy that economists have expressed concerns about, linking them to increased economic risks and potential impacts on future inflation. Regarding monetary policy, Trump has questioned the independence of the Federal Reserve, suggesting the president should have a role in its decisions, a stance that economists view as potentially undermining the central bank's credibility in fighting inflation. While specific new proposals on taxation and government spending are not detailed in the provided information, past tax and spending bills under his administration were a source of concern for economists regarding potential economic damage. Information on his current specific policies for job creation is not detailed in the provided sources.

**Social Policy: These policies focus on the well-being and welfare of the population. This broad category includes areas like healthcare, education, social security programs, housing, and policies aimed at addressing poverty and inequality.:**
Donald Trump's social policy stances emphasize deregulation, market-driven approaches, and a shift of control from the federal government to states and individuals. 

On **healthcare**, Trump has consistently advocated for repealing and replacing the Affordable Care Act (ACA) with a market-driven system focused on competition and innovation. His proposed policies would likely reduce health insurance enrollment and increase the federal deficit compared to the ACA. He has reversed Biden-era ACA enrollment expansions and his administration has endorsed significant cuts to Medicaid, despite previous assurances not to reduce funding, which could shift financial burdens to states and jeopardize coverage for millions. His stance on drug pricing initiatives is nuanced, with some reversals of Biden-era policies, suggesting a lean towards limiting government intervention while still acknowledging the need for cost control.

In **education**, Trump proposes closing the Department of Education and returning control to the states. He is a strong proponent of universal school choice, empowering parents to choose public, private, charter, or faith-based schools for their children, and emphasizes parental rights. He has ordered cuts to federal education funding, which would likely impact low-income communities, and seeks to eliminate diversity, equity, and inclusion programs from school curricula.

Regarding **Social Security**, Trump has pledged not to cut benefits for Social Security, Medicare, or Medicaid, stating he made a "sacred pledge" to protect the program. He aims to lower inflation, which would affect Cost of Living Adjustments (COLAs), and has proposed eliminating taxes on Social Security benefits. His administration has also focused on removing fraud, waste, and abuse from the Social Security system.

For **housing**, Trump's administration has proposed significant changes, including a two-year limit on federal rental assistance programs like Section 8 vouchers, which could lead to many households, particularly those with children, losing benefits. An executive order on "restoring equality" is viewed by some as a step towards repealing the Fair Housing Act by disregarding the disparate impact standard. His approach to homelessness includes dismantling the U.S. Interagency Council on Homelessness (USICH) and potentially addressing the issue through privately operated tent cities. While his tax and spending bill includes incentives for developers to build affordable rental housing, it also aims to reduce the number of Americans on government programs like Medicaid.

On **poverty and inequality**, Trump's administration has proposed major cuts to social programs such as SNAP, TANF, SSI, and Medicaid, which are critical for poverty reduction. He has supported a House version of the Farm Bill that would cut benefits for millions. Critics argue his policies, including sweeping tariffs, could increase the cost of basic necessities and exacerbate inequality, effectively harming the working class. Additionally, his immigration policies, such as suspending refugee resettlement and reinstating the "Remain in Mexico" policy, have been linked to deepening inequality.

**Environmental & Energy Policy: This category deals with the protection of the environment, the management of natural resources, and the development and regulation of energy sources. This includes policies related to climate change, conservation, pollution control, and the transition to renewable energy.:**
Donald Trump's environmental and energy policy stances are characterized by a strong emphasis on fossil fuel development and a skeptical approach to renewable energy and climate change initiatives. His administration has consistently aimed to support the fossil fuel industry, issuing executive orders to boost its development. Conversely, he has expressed direct opposition to renewable energy sources like wind and solar, stating, "We will not approve wind or farmer destroying Solar," and has taken actions to slow their growth and end related incentives. A core tenet of his policy is the removal of what he deems "burdensome environmental regulations." He has also stepped back from actions on climate change and environmental justice initiatives. A stated goal is to "Restore American Energy Dominance," which in his policy framework, typically translates to increased domestic production of oil, gas, and coal.

**Foreign Affairs & National Security: These policies govern a nation's interactions with other countries and its defense. This includes diplomacy, immigration policy, defense spending, intelligence activities, international trade agreements, and responses to global threats.:**
Donald Trump's foreign affairs and national security policies are largely defined by an "America First" doctrine, prioritizing national interests and bilateral agreements over multilateral ones. His approach is often characterized by unpredictability, a preference for "deals" over traditional diplomacy, and a willingness to use economic leverage, such as tariffs, to achieve objectives. On international trade, he has pursued protectionist policies, imposing tariffs and renegotiating agreements like NAFTA (now USMCA), while also striking new bilateral deals. In terms of immigration, his stance is marked by aggressive enforcement, including efforts to remove noncitizens, limit humanitarian relief, and secure the southern border, advocating for a "merit-based" system. Regarding defense spending, Trump supports a "peace through strength" philosophy, proposing significant increases to the defense budget for military modernization and pushing NATO allies to raise their defense contributions. His national security strategy emphasizes homeland security and confronting global threats, particularly taking a strong stance against Iran and supporting Israel and Saudi Arabia, while also focusing on combating terrorist groups like ISIS. In the realm of intelligence activities, he has been described as less engaged with traditional intelligence briefings and has been accused of politicizing intelligence agencies. Future plans, such as Project 2025, suggest a potential restructuring to centralize more power within the intelligence community.

**Candidate Name: Kamala Harris**

**Economic Policy: This category includes government actions aimed at influencing the economy's performance and stability. It covers areas like taxation, government spending, trade regulations, monetary policy, jobs, and inflation.:**
Kamala Harris's economic policy centers on what she calls an "opportunity economy," aiming to lower costs for middle-class families and enhance financial security. Her proposals include tax increases for businesses and high earners, aligning with much of President Biden's FY 2025 budget. Specifically, she supports raising the corporate income tax rate from 21% to 28%. While her economic agenda emphasizes tax incentives and proposals to reduce costs for food, housing, healthcare, and childcare, many specific details regarding her tax policies remain unspecified, including her stance on the expiration of the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (TCJA). The potential long-run economic effects of her tax proposals, as analyzed by some, include a decrease in GDP, GNP, capital stock, wage rates, and full-time equivalent jobs. The full scope of her spending priorities and their impact on the federal deficit are also not yet fully detailed.

**Social Policy: These policies focus on the well-being and welfare of the population. This broad category includes areas like healthcare, education, social security programs, housing, and policies aimed at addressing poverty and inequality.:**
Kamala Harris's social policy platform focuses on expanding access, reducing costs, and promoting equity across various sectors. In **healthcare**, she advocates for strengthening the Affordable Care Act (ACA), expanding Medicaid, and broadening traditional Medicare to include dental, vision, and hearing services. She proposes a "Medicare at Home" plan to cover home care, aims to lower prescription drug prices by extending Medicare's negotiated price caps, cap out-of-pocket drug expenses, and increase pricing transparency. Harris also supports canceling medical debt, strengthening women's health, and protecting reproductive rights, though she no longer supports Medicare for All, focusing instead on marketplace plans.

For **education**, Harris champions universal pre-kindergarten and debt-free college. She supports expanding career and technical education, reducing the emphasis on standardized testing, and improving teachers' working conditions and pay. She opposes eliminating the Department of Education and seeks to strengthen funding for low-income and disabled students. Harris is also against book bans and advocates for acknowledging the nation's full history.

Regarding **Social Security**, Harris strongly opposes any cuts to benefits and aims to strengthen the program's solvency, potentially by raising taxes on incomes above $400,000. She has supported the Social Security Expansion Act and seeks to increase benefits, including raising the special minimum benefit for low-earning workers to 125% of the federal poverty level. She has also supported changes to the Social Security COLA calculation to potentially lead to higher increases.

In **housing**, Harris has made affordability a central theme, proposing the construction of 3 million new housing units within four years. Her plans include incentivizing multi-family housing, opening federal lands for residential development, and offering tax incentives for builders of low-cost "starter" homes. She also advocates for $25,000 in downpayment support for first-time homeowners and aims to create a fairer rental market by strengthening renter protections, holding corporate landlords accountable, and passing the "Stop Predatory Investing Act" to remove tax benefits for large corporate landlords. Additionally, she supports expanding rental assistance and increasing housing supply for people experiencing homelessness.

On **poverty and inequality**, Harris has proposed economic policies to empower the middle class, focusing on food prices, taxes, housing, and medical costs. Her tax plan includes a significant expansion of the Child Tax Credit, targeted at those most in need, and proposes raising taxes on well-off households by reforming Medicare taxes, scaling back tax breaks for capital gains and dividends for those earning over $1 million, and reforming corporate taxes to address income and racial inequality. In 2019, she proposed the LIFT Act, which would provide a refundable tax credit for working adults and couples. Some sources also associate her with support for universal basic income (UBI).

Kamala Harris supports clean energy and cutting planet-warming emissions. As a U.S. Senator, she co-sponsored the Green New Deal, which aims for a transition to 100% clean energy within a decade. During her 2020 presidential campaign, she stated her opposition to fracking and offshore drilling and pledged to ban fossil fuel leases on public lands if elected president. As Vice President, she has supported significant government investments in clean energy and climate initiatives, often framing climate change as an economic issue due to rising insurance costs from extreme weather.
