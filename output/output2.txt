**Candidate Name: Zohran Mamdani**

**Economic Policy: This category includes government actions aimed at influencing the economy's performance and stability. It covers areas like taxation, government spending, trade regulations, monetary policy, jobs, and inflation.:**
Zohran Mamdani's economic policy stances are centered on increasing affordability and shifting wealth and power, particularly in New York City. Key proposals include implementing higher tax rates for high-income individuals and corporations, with a specific mention of a proposed 51% increase in the NYC tax rate for those earning $1 million or more annually. He advocates for tighter rent control and government housing subsidies to address housing affordability. Mamdani also supports a phased increase in the minimum wage, aiming for $30 an hour by 2028. Furthermore, his economic vision includes the establishment of government-run grocery stores and government-subsidized daycare. He emphasizes leveraging community wealth-building strategies such as cooperatives, Community Land Trusts (CLTs), and public banking to empower communities. Additionally, Mamdani promotes a 'green economy' plan that not only focuses on reducing emissions but also aims to make the city more livable and affordable, advocating for a rapid green transition and highlighting the hidden costs of climate inaction.

**Social Policy: These policies focus on the well-being and welfare of the population. This broad category includes areas like healthcare, education, social security programs, housing, and policies aimed at addressing poverty and inequality.:**
Zohran Mamdani, a New York State Assemblymember and democratic socialist, advocates for a robust social policy agenda focused on the well-being and welfare of the population. In healthcare, he rejects Medicare Advantage and higher copays for in-service workers, instead proposing partnerships with workers and unions to tackle the for-profit healthcare system and reduce costs. He also opposes legislation that would cut funding for vital public healthcare programs, particularly those benefiting disabled, elderly, and low-income individuals. For education, Mamdani champions fully funded public schools with equitable resource distribution, strong after-school programs, mental health counselors, nurses, appropriate class sizes, and integrated student bodies. He also proposes creating car-free "School Streets" to enhance safety and reduce pollution, and addresses student homelessness by expanding the "Every Child and Family Is Known" pilot program. He is critical of the current mayoral control over schools, advocating for a more participatory educational system. On housing, Mamdani supports freezing rent increases on rent-stabilized apartments and addresses student homelessness. More broadly, to combat poverty and inequality, he has actively fought for the working class, including securing debt relief for taxi drivers, increasing funding for subway services and a fare-free bus pilot, and organizing against a proposed power plant. He also proposes opening city-run grocery stores and emphasizes the importance of a strong social safety net and affordable cost of living, frequently highlighting issues like child hunger and student homelessness in New York City.

**Environmental & Energy Policy: This category deals with the protection of the environment, the management of natural resources, and the development and regulation of energy sources. This includes policies related to climate change, conservation, pollution control, and the transition to renewable energy.:**
Zohran Mamdani is a strong advocate for progressive environmental and energy policies, emphasizing a transition to publicly owned renewable energy and a significant reduction in reliance on fossil fuels. He has actively supported initiatives like the Build Public Renewables Act and campaigned against new fossil fuel infrastructure, such as the Astoria peaker plant. A core tenet of his approach is linking climate policy with economic affordability, consistently opposing utility rate hikes and arguing that public ownership of the energy system can ensure lower costs, long-term resilience, and decisions made in the public interest. Mamdani views the climate crisis not solely as an environmental issue but also as a social and economic one, advocating for a "just transition" that provides renewable power, democratic control over energy, and relief from high energy costs. His vision integrates sustainability with broader societal benefits, connecting clean air with classroom quality and carbon reduction with economic recovery, and he proposes pragmatic solutions like fare-free transit and green school retrofits.

**Economic Policy: This category includes government actions aimed at influencing the economy's performance and stability. It covers areas like taxation, government spending, trade regulations, monetary policy, jobs, and inflation.:**
Curtis Sliwa, a Republican candidate for Mayor of New York City, has expressed opposition to proposals that would tax the city's wealthy to subsidize transit costs. He has criticized such economic recovery plans, particularly those put forth by his rival, Eric Adams. Sliwa has also stated that new real estate development should not require approvals beyond those currently existing under the city land use review process, and he views the current practices in this area as corrupt.
