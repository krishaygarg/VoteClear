**Candidate Name: Winsome Earle-Sears**

**Economic Policy: This category includes government actions aimed at influencing the economy's performance and stability. It covers areas like taxation, government spending, trade regulations, monetary policy, jobs, and inflation.:**
Winsome Earle-Sears' economic policy stances are largely characterized by a focus on traditional energy sources, opposition to certain environmental regulations she views as economically detrimental, and support for policies that limit union influence. She has been a vocal critic of policies aimed at reducing carbon emissions and transitioning to renewable energy, arguing for an "all-of-the-above approach" that includes continued investment in fossil fuels. Earle-Sears has specifically labeled the Virginia Clean Economy Act as "disastrous," attributing higher electrical bills to it, and actively worked to withdraw Virginia from the Regional Greenhouse Gas Initiative (RGGI), which she refers to as an "energy tax." Regarding labor policy, she has criticized federal legislation that would have overridden Virginia's "right-to-work" law, indicating her support for policies that prohibit mandatory union membership or dues as a condition of employment. While generally described as an advocate for "economic opportunity," specific details on other economic policies such as taxation, government spending (beyond energy-related aspects), trade regulations, or monetary policy are not explicitly detailed in the provided information.

**Social Policy: These policies focus on the well-being and welfare of the population. This broad category includes areas like healthcare, education, social security programs, housing, and policies aimed at addressing poverty and inequality.:**
Winsome Earle-Sears, an immigrant from Jamaica and a devout Christian, has served in Virginia's House of Delegates and is the first Black woman to hold statewide office in Virginia as Lieutenant Governor. Her social policy stances are generally conservative. She has expressed strong opposition to abortion, previously calling it "genocide" and vowing to "do everything in my power" to end it in Virginia. On same-sex marriage, she is "morally opposed" to the legislation protecting marriage equality, a stance that differs from Governor Youngkin's. While housing policy is a significant issue in Virginia, Earle-Sears has not specifically addressed it on the campaign trail. Regarding inequality, she emphasizes looking forward and celebrating the achievements of disadvantaged people, advocating for equality of opportunity over equality of outcomes, and equipping students to seize opportunities.

**Environmental & Energy Policy: This category deals with the protection of the environment, the management of natural resources, and the development and regulation of energy sources. This includes policies related to climate change, conservation, pollution control, and the transition to renewable energy.:**
Winsome Earle-Sears advocates for an "all-of-the-above" energy strategy for Virginia, which includes a mix of fossil fuels (coal and natural gas), nuclear power, and renewable energy sources. Her primary focus is on achieving energy independence and ensuring affordable energy rates for consumers through expanded production capacity. She has expressed skepticism regarding a rapid transition solely to renewable energy, citing concerns about the limitations of battery storage and its ability to sustain the economy. Earle-Sears has been critical of policies aimed at reducing carbon emissions, specifically calling the Virginia Clean Economy Act (VCEA) "disastrous" and attributing higher electricity bills to it. The VCEA mandates a complete transition to renewable energy production for utilities by 2050. Furthermore, she supported and partnered in the effort to withdraw Virginia from the Regional Greenhouse Gas Initiative (RGGI), which she referred to as an "energy tax."

**Foreign Affairs & National Security: These policies govern a nation's interactions with other countries and its defense. This includes diplomacy, immigration policy, defense spending, intelligence activities, international trade agreements, and responses to global threats.:**
Winsome Earle-Sears emphasizes strict enforcement of immigration laws and close collaboration with federal authorities, including Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE). She supports the expansion of 287(g) agreements, which empower local law enforcement to assist in federal immigration enforcement. Earle-Sears views immigration primarily through a public safety lens, stating that immigrants who commit crimes should be deported. On national security, she links it to energy independence, advocating for a diverse energy strategy that includes "clean coal, oil, renewables, nuclear, [and] natural gas," arguing that a sole reliance on renewables is not a viable national security approach given the global energy landscape and the actions of other nations.

**Candidate Name: Abigail Spanberger**

**Economic Policy: This category includes government actions aimed at influencing the economy's performance and stability. It covers areas like taxation, government spending, trade regulations, monetary policy, jobs, and inflation.:**
Abigail Spanberger's economic policy centers around her "Growing Virginia Plan," which aims to bolster Virginia's economy and create job opportunities across the state. A core tenet of her approach is a focus on tax cuts and a commitment to scrutinizing government expenditures to eliminate wasteful spending. She advocates for streamlining or eliminating business regulations, asserting that taxpayers and businesses are better equipped to manage their finances and operations than the government.

Key pillars of her economic strategy include expanding workforce development to prepare Virginians for modern jobs, increasing business investment, and enhancing trade opportunities for Virginia-based producers. She has expressed concerns about job growth in Virginia and the impact of federal cuts and trade policies on the state's economy. Spanberger also emphasizes addressing affordability for Virginians, linking it to broader economic stability and the ability of families to thrive, specifically mentioning affordable healthcare, childcare, and housing. She believes that a skilled workforce, reliable infrastructure, and strong public schools are crucial factors for attracting and growing businesses in Virginia.

**Environmental & Energy Policy: This category deals with the protection of the environment, the management of natural resources, and the development and regulation of energy sources. This includes policies related to climate change, conservation, pollution control, and the transition to renewable energy.:**
Abigail Spanberger views climate change as both an opportunity and a challenge for Virginia, emphasizing energy use and conservation as key issues. She advocates for Virginia to become a "dominant" force in renewable energy, supporting robust development in this sector and maintaining clean energy mandates, including the Virginia Clean Economy Act (VCEA). Her energy platform also includes the continued use of natural gas "as needed," indicating a pragmatic approach to energy transition. Spanberger's plan focuses on demand-side management and consumer-focused energy efficiency measures, and she supports significant investment in battery storage and "virtual power plants." Additionally, she has expressed support for other advanced energy technologies such as nuclear (specifically small modular reactors), hydrogen, fusion, and geothermal. A notable aspect of her policy is the proposal for energy-reliant data centers to contribute to upgrades for the electrical grid.

**Foreign Affairs & National Security: These policies govern a nation's interactions with other countries and its defense. This includes diplomacy, immigration policy, defense spending, intelligence activities, international trade agreements, and responses to global threats.:**
Abigail Spanberger, a former CIA officer, emphasizes her national security background as a testament to her toughness and thoroughness. While national security and foreign policy are not typically top concerns in American elections, her experience is presented as a valuable asset. On immigration, Spanberger advocates for comprehensive reform, citing the Farm Workforce Modernization Act, the Dignity Act, and a bipartisan U.S. Senate compromise as potential solutions to what she describes as a "broken and outdated" system. She voted against the Laken Riley Act, which mandates ICE detention for immigrants accused of certain crimes, due to concerns about imposing costly burdens on localities and stripping away due process. If elected Governor of Virginia, she has also stated her intention to undo Governor Glenn Youngkin's February immigration order.
